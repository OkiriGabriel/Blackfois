Difference between mock up and logo design

by Famewo oyinola in Design

A lot of Graphic Designers struggle each day in terms of making their clients understand a simpleconcept. “Photorealistic mockups are not the real deal.” Some graphic designers even make the mistake of mixing up mockup designs and logo designs. Let’s start from their definitions; MOCKUP DESIGNS are just part of the designer's presentation purpose.
It is a scale or full-sized model of a design or device, used for teaching, demonstration, design evaluation, promotion, and other purposes. A mockup is a prototype if it provides at least part of the functionality of a system and enables testing of a design. Mock-ups are used by designers mainly to acquire feedback from users. (Wikipedia) A LOGO DESIGN on the other hand, is a recognizable graphic design element, often including a name, symbol or trademark, representing an organization or product. It is the one to be finalized, edited, and to be handed over to the client.
While the term may appear complicated, photorealistic mockups are simple images and ready-made Photoshop mockups that graphic designers use for presentation purposes only. Sometimes it’s difficult to make clients understand how their final logo design will appear in physical rendering, signage or when it’s engraved on aglass door are some of the reasons why a mock-up design is needed.This is one of the main reasons why graphic designers go that extra step into deciphering the design to enhance the client’s understanding. Presenting the logo in a realistic real-world scenario is what we call the photorealistic mockup.
I hope we all understand clearly now that a mockup design isn’t a logo and a logo isn’t a mockup design.
